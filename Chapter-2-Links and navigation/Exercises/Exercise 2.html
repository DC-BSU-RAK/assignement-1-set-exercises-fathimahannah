The Summer I Turned Pretty

The Summer I Turned Pretty is a heartwarming and emotional coming-of-age drama that explores the complexities of adolescence, first love, and family. At the center of the story is Isabel "Belly" Conklin, who spends her summers at Cousins Beach with her family and the Fisher boys—Conrad and Jeremiah. This summer is different. Belly isn’t the little girl she used to be. She's grown into someone who finally turns heads—and it’s changing everything.

As Belly navigates her feelings and the newfound attention, she finds herself in a complicated love triangle between the brooding, emotionally closed-off Conrad and the vibrant, easygoing Jeremiah. But this isn’t just a story about romance—it's also about loss, identity, self-discovery, and the special bonds that tie people together during life’s most transformative moments. The show's emotional depth and coastal summer vibe make it a favorite among fans of YA dramas.

Fan Reviews

Amanda Ibe: "I absolutely loved this show. The story felt so real and heartfelt, and I connected with Belly’s character in so many ways. Her emotional journey was beautiful to watch. The chemistry between the characters, especially Belly and Jeremiah, was amazing. I’m definitely team Jelly! This series made me cry, smile, and feel all the feelings. I already started reading the books because I couldn’t get enough!"

Evelyn “Eve” Webb: "The Summer I Turned Pretty is more than just a teen romance series. It’s a beautiful, emotional ride that hits you right in the heart. I’ve rewatched it three times and it still gives me chills. The cast is incredibly talented and the writing captures the essence of growing up so well. It even inspired me to pursue acting! There’s something magical about how this show makes you feel seen. It’s comfort, nostalgia, and warmth all rolled into one."

Ranyoun Rania: "I started this series just out of curiosity and ended up binge-watching all the episodes in one night. I couldn’t stop. The characters are so well developed, and the story is deeply emotional and relatable. I loved seeing Belly grow through her experiences. The love triangle adds tension, but it’s the personal growth and family themes that really stand out. Highly recommend it to anyone who loves heartfelt stories with emotional depth."
